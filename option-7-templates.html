Big-4 firm templates

Each template encodes the engagement firm's tickmark conventions, section ordering, narrative voice, and evidence formatting requirements. The FDE pod applies a template to a workpaper to keep deliverables consistent with what the auditor expects to see.

Why this matters in Agent Commodity Hell. Foundation models do not know what tickmark vocabulary the Helios engagement team expects. They do not know that EY frames procedures risk-first while PwC frames them sample-first. They will draft something plausible-looking that fails the senior-reviewer pass. The templates are the firm-specific style guide that the FDE attaches when applying a draft, and the scorer enforces. That gap — between plausible-looking and accepted-without-rework — is what the Practice arm is selling.
